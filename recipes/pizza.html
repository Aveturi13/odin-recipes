Detroit Style Pizza

Description

Forget the old Chicago vs. NY debate! America's best pizza could very well be Detroit-style pizza. Even though it's often referred to as a 'deep dish,' I don't think that accurately captures the essence of this crispy, crusty, crazy-good slice. It has flavor elements of a slightly charred thin-crust pizza, with the texture of light, airy focaccia. Plus, if you use the properly shaped pan, the edges of your crust get wonderfully crunchy, making for a truly unique experience.

While this recipe is awesome, I don't think its vegeterian friendly as it contains pepperoni! So I've made some slight modifications.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup of warm water
  • 1 package of active dry yeast
  • 1 tsp white sugar
  • 3 tsp olive oil
  • 1 tsp kosher salt
  • 2.5 cups bread flour
  • 1 jar of marinara sauce
  • 1 tsp of red pepper flakes
  • 1 tsp garlic powder
  • Mozzarella cheese (shredded)

Extra Toppings (to make it vegeterian-friendly!)

  • Jalapenos
  • sundried tomatos
  • Onions (red and white)
  • Bell Peppers - can use red, yellow and green peppers to make it more colorful!
  • Zucchini
  • and if you're courageous, try pineapple!

Steps

  1. Pour warm water into the bowl of stand mixer; mix in yeast and sugar and let dissolve. Add 2 teaspoons olive oil, salt, and bread flour. Knead mixture together with a dough hook attachment until dough is very smooth, soft, and elastic.
  2. Drizzle remaining olive oil over the bottom of a 10x14-inch Detroit-style pizza pan and spread around with your fingers. Place dough in the center; pull and stretch into a roughly rectangular shape with oiled fingers. Cover and let rise until doubled in volume, about 1 hour. Prepare sauce and cheese in the meantime.
  3. Combine marinara sauce, oregano, red pepper flakes, and garlic powder in a saucepan over medium-low heat. Simmer to fully hydrate the oregano and garlic, about 15 minutes.
  4. Preheat the oven to 500 to 550 degrees F (260 to 288 degrees C).
  5. Rub fingertips with some olive oil from the pan. Press out air from the dough while stretching and pushing into a rectangle that goes all the way to the edges of the pan. Stretch up the sides about 1/2 inch or so.
  6. Add the vegetable toppings generously on the dough and then cover with the mozzarella cheese.
  7. Bake in the preheated oven until pizza is somewhere between golden brown and golden black, about 15 minutes. Let cool for 5 minutes.
  8. Very carefully slide pizza onto a cutting board using a spatula. Cut into individual pieces using the lines of sauce as a guide.
